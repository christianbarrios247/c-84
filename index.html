Project 2:
Thermal Analysis & Computational Fluid Dynamics

The objective of the simulations was to determine whether enough air was going to cool down the electronics within the compartment box. Since the competition is located in Utah, the air cooling would have to be working at its maximum. Another thing that had to be verified was if the electronics were going to function in the heat. Utilizing Flow Simulation on Solidworks, I was able to determine the airflow through the electronic compartment box. Different variations of the CFD was created using different fan placements using configurations. It was determined that the fans located opposite each other was more effective when minimizing hot spots within the compartment box. Next, a thermal analysis was conducted to determine if each electronic was going to overheat due to the environment temperature. Once the simulation was completed, it was determined that each component was going to operate within their operating temperature ranges that were found in the data sheets.

Project 3:
Propeller Design

This project was to create a propeller design for ENGR 1500: Introduction to Engineering. In this competition, a group of students created their own propeller designs. Research was done to determine how many blades would be sufficient for speed and power. I modeled my design using Solidworks to create the model with different blade count, area, thickness, and angle of twist. In my design, 4 blades were included since it adds durabilty however, it will lose speed. Since the propeller was going to be tested in water on a ROV, I made sure to it was gonna be durable since the product was going to be 3D printed.

Project 4
L'SPACE Mission Concept Academy

For this project, a rover was designed using Siemens NX to make a CAD model that was created to perform certain tasks on the moon. In this mission concept, the rover is suppose to withstand moonquakes, able to drive around the terrain without any trouble, and lastly to withstand the radiation emmitted by the sun. On the CAD model, power tools were also added onto the model to perform the requirements to drill onto the surface of the moon and drill and collect rocks. In this model, I assisted with the design of the cameras and the suspension system. The suspension system that I was able to create was the Rocker-Bogie because it would be able to drive around a rocky terrain.
